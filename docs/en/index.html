Memory of the cat Myata

🇺🇸 Machine translation (AI) from Russian.

Hello, my name is Myata! 🐈‍⬛#

And this is my welcoming purr for you:

“Myata” is pronounced “mee-AH-tah” (stress on second syllable). “Mee” like “meet”. “Ah” like “father”. “Tah” like “taco”. “Myata” (Мята) in Russian means “mint” — the herb.

Once I got lost and couldn’t find my way home, but I managed to find a new shelter and a new life among the people who love me.

This new life is what I want to tell you about on this page.

Introduction#

02.08.2020#

August. City of Novosibirsk, Akademgorodok.

I have been living on the street for several days. A woman named Alyona has been feeding me. On another night, I sit near the entrances of old Khrushchyovkas and meow plaintively. I feel lonely and scared.

Suddenly, a young couple – Vita and Sasha – notice me.

It is clear that Sasha initially refuses to take me, coming up with various reasons, but then he agrees. Thus, I ended up in an apartment where I found a new family.

That night, when the guys found me, they had a cat named Shurfik, a sphinx breed, which their friends had left with them for a few days. Just before they took me, by a happy coincidence, Shurfik’s owners returned and took him back. They came straight from the airport to pick him up.

These are my first awkward photos before I found my new family:

03.08.2020#

Guys, of course, fed me. It was Shurfik’s food. They even made me a little bed.

I felt calm, but the guys didn’t even have a litter box. They crumpled up some newspaper for me, and I used it as a toilet. Later, they bought me the necessary toilet accessories.

These are some of my first photos at the guys’ home:

08.08.2020#

I am a beautiful kitty, but my previous guardians apparently did not look for me. This hurt my self-esteem a bit.

The guys were looking for them. They put up flyers in several blocks nearby, published my photos online, but they couldn’t find them. Sasha didn’t want to give me away just like that anymore.

In the photo, I am looking at Vita’s bicycle. Sasha also had a bicycle. In general, if it weren’t for those bicycles, they probably wouldn’t have met me, because they went outside at night to ride them.

It was already clear: I was staying. Now the guys were taking me to the veterinary clinic. There, they tried to determine my age. They agreed that I am about 5 years old. My fangs were slightly chipped, and there were scratches on my ears — only these signs hinted that I was definitely not 2 years old.

I didn’t need to be sterilized; I was already sterilized. The mark on one of my ears might indicate that I was sterilized as a street cat. I had never kept a diary before and can’t tell you about it.

Akademgorodok#

04.12.2020#

My cat-like essence demands certain rituals from me. For example, I am forced to sharpen my claws. And so that I don’t accidentally fall in love with doing this on the furniture, the guys provided me with a scratching post.

It was a terrible scratching post. It was very unstable. A little later, I was allowed to sharpen my claws on the IKEA wooden shelf. And it was wonderful.

05.01.2021#

Time passed. And here I am in the new 2021 year. The guys celebrated it at a vegan table in a very small circle. And for several days in a row, I portrayed a little gift.

08.01.2021#

It was the time of the raging COVID-19, but personally, I was happy.

I was almost never left home alone because everyone was staying at home. I liked it.

14.03.2021#

My tummy has never been petted so often. And even though I never allowed anyone to hold me in their arms, I really loved affection. Even if you came to visit, I would let you cuddle me. The main thing is, don’t pick me up.

03.04.2021#

I hinted to the guys that I love drinking tap water, and they bought me a modern automatic water dispenser. I don’t like standing water in a bowl. I prefer to eat from a bowl.

25.04.2021#

I play with different toys. I love to knock pens off the table, I love all sorts of homemade strange things that Sasha constantly builds.

And that old clumsy cat scratcher has been slightly modified. It was tied to the bed. This made it a bit more useful than before, but only for a short time.

07.05.2021#

Sasha is working, Vita is drawing. I’m just resting. Time flies quickly.

That year I had few photos. But I managed to find something. Here, for example, I fell asleep right after listening to popular cat hits.

15.12.2021#

To keep an eye on me, Sasha installed a special video camera in the house. It could record both day and night. And if the guys were away, they could always check how I was doing.

But there are also downsides to such control. For example, I was caught at night eating.

26.12.2021#

Overall, I am an advanced kitty and I approve of new technologies. But I still haven’t decided how to feel about these cameras. On one hand — safety, cool, on the other — violation of privacy.

Judge for yourself: you decide to wash your little butt, and you’ll be captured and the materials sent to a Chinese server.

08.01.2022#

The year 2021 has come to an end. The guys certainly celebrated it. I won’t overload you with content, but I’ll tell you that I celebrated it without them. Sasha and Vita went to the village, and I was given a nanny. She came to feed me and play with me. But on New Year’s Eve, of course, no one stayed with me. I just slept under the sounds of fireworks.

But now it’s already 2022. In a way, I burst into it on all four of my paws! It’s a pity there was no Christmas tree. Maybe I would have knocked it over for variety.

30.01.2022#

Once, Vita brought home a broken old chair from the street and fixed it. She painted it and replaced the fabric. The chair, as you can see, became designer. I couldn’t miss such an opportunity and took a photo for memory.

Later, this chair will be taken to a photo studio. Who knows, maybe it is still somewhere, and actors or actresses are taking pictures with it.

On my face, there is serenity.

24.02.2022#

Closed doors are very bad. Doors should always be open. Rarely, but I managed to get someone to let me into the entrance of the building. I wanted all the doors in this world to be open, but alas, I returned home unsuccessful.

On this day, the war began. The life of a Siberian kitty will soon change again.

I haven’t told you yet, but during the first years of my life with the guys, I hardly had a voice. I meowed in a whisper. Later, I would learn to meow loudly, but in this video, you can hear what my voice was like before.

Emigration#

21.03.2022#

Sasha stopped feeling safe due to the arbitrariness of the Russian authorities and changes in society. He decided to move abroad and take all of us with him. Me and Vita. He absolutely did not want to leave us behind.

In connection with the planned departure, the guys began to sell their belongings. Even this IKEA mattress, next to which I am playing, was sold.

03.04.2022#

I got all the necessary vaccinations and a passport. A couple of days before the flight, the veterinary clinic issued me a special certificate that allowed me to pass all customs and proceed to my destination.

I hadn’t told anyone about this before, but because of all this bureaucracy, the guys even had to give up their tickets and buy new ones with departure dates later than they had originally planned. Otherwise, I wouldn’t have been allowed to leave due to non-compliance with quarantine.

So it wasn’t easy, but we managed. In this photo, I’m in the carrier that was bought specifically for the flight.

05.04.2022#

So I found myself abroad for the first time. First, we flew to Tashkent. I am very glad that I was allowed into the cabin of the plane. And although I didn’t really enjoy the flight, I am very grateful to the flight attendant for offering to sit separately, where there was plenty of space for both me and the guys. It was “Uzbekistan Airways.”

We will spend very little time in Tashkent and will fly further. It seems the guys liked it there. I was excited about having to fly on a plane again.

Arrival#

08.04.2022#

From Tashkent, we flew to Tbilisi. This is a city where we will live for a long time.

If only you knew how happy I was to be in the hotel after these flights. But the hotel is not the final destination of our stay. While I was lounging in bed, the guys were looking for us a new home.

14.05.2022#

As soon as we found a new apartment, we didn’t even have time to live there before we went to Batumi. Our friends invited us over, and since there was no one to leave me with for now, our whole friendly family set off again.

In Batumi, I lived in a very tall building, and I wasn’t allowed on the balcony.

But I did ride a train for the first time. It wasn’t so bad. I was often taken out of the carrier, and I explored everything around.

Then, of course, we returned to Tbilisi.

Lisi#

10.06.2022#

It was nice and spacious in the new house in Tbilisi. I liked running around there. Nearby was Lake Lisi. The guys sometimes went there for walks.

I was still given a lot of attention and cuddled. I have very good memories of that period. After the old Khrushchyovka, of course, this apartment seemed divine.

04.07.2022#

Once I thought about the journey I had taken, and it left me disheartened. Could a little Siberian cat have imagined that she would live in sunny Georgia?

28.07.2022#

But life went on, and I was watching my figure. I always eat in moderation and never overeat. I eat little by little, but many times a day.

Mtskheta str.#

17.09.2022#

A little later we moved to a new home, where there was a fireplace and a small yard!

And although the interior was not very modern, I could really pick up speed when I ran, thanks to the huge area of the apartment.

10.10.2022#

Did I miss life in Russia? No, but I was worried that my favorite people had left their past life. Perhaps it was just starting to get better for them there.

11.11.2022#

I have already told you that it’s better not to pick me up. And that’s true. But sometimes I make an exception and come to sit on laps myself. I only started doing this in Georgia. I didn’t do it before.

06.01.2023#

Look at me, what a beauty I am.

Everyone always pays attention to the color of my eyes. In this photo, they seem green, but in reality, they are closer to mint. Maybe that’s why they named me Myata (that’s mint in English).

Meanwhile, we have crossed the threshold of the new year. It’s already 2023.

09.01.2023#

One could not rest on their laurels. Sasha worked a lot, and I helped him as much as I could. I often came to him and sat on the table to the right. It seems he liked it. I purred and supported him. Then he fed me.

And here I am sitting not on the table. But I still support him a lot.

17.02.2023#

Despite the political situation and the ongoing war, I felt quite comfortable with the guys. Sasha continued to make me various strange toys that I could play with. And it always made me happy.

26.02.2023#

A couple of times I got sick, and we went to the veterinarians. I don’t like going to the doctors, but during those two times they helped me a lot, and I am glad.

Any trip is a big stress for me. Just look at these worried eyes.

01.03.2023#

Meet Yulia! She was taking care of me while the guys went on vacation.

She knows that I shouldn’t be picked up, but she somehow needed to show them that I am alive and well.

Yulia is great, and I felt comfortable with her. Thanks to her, the guys were able to visit cities like Ankara, Dubai, Abu Dhabi.

22.04.2023#

Remember, I mentioned the courtyard. Well, there were actually two of them. In one, a thrush chick once lived, and in the other, stray cats would come by. I shared my food with the kitties.

Looking out the window is one of my favorite hobbies. There’s always something worthy of my attention happening out there.

16.05.2023#

After all, it’s nice to just lie on the windowsill.

24.05.2023#

In addition to my window hobby, I engage in backyard hiking. For this, I have a special harness so I don’t get lost again. I enjoy walking, but only if it’s a calm and quiet place. Otherwise, I get scared of everything.

There are many interesting smells outside. I love to explore them all.

Fleas#

06.09.2023#

While walking in the yard, where sometimes stray cats roam, I picked up my first fleas.

Do you see the collar on me? Yes! It’s a flea collar. Which, by the way, didn’t help me much. In the end, I got rid of the fleas with a magic pill.

In the video, I still have fleas. And in the photo — I’m already without fleas. It’s much, much better without them.

Cholecystitis#

29.09.2023#

And even though I always ate properly and led an active lifestyle, I still developed gallstones.

This seems to be my very first serious visit to the clinic. I had no appetite at all, and I had to get an IV drip.

03.10.2023#

I can imagine how the guys felt spending several days driving with me to the clinic and sitting there for almost an hour each time. I’m sure they are not thrilled about these trips either.

This went on for some time. Sasha learned to give me injections at home for my sake, and I must admit that it’s better than trips to the clinic. I feel much more comfortable at home.

18.11.2023#

But I have no reason to complain. They treated my cholecystitis, changed my food, and I started goofing around again.

Didi Digomi#

25.11.2023#

At some point (quite suddenly), we had to move out of the apartment that had a yard. We stayed in a hotel for a few days to find housing.

The hotel was inexpensive but nice. I liked it there. We stayed there for a few days.

28.11.2023#

We left the previous apartment so quickly that we forgot an important part of the water dispenser, so at the hotel I drank water from the shower. You know, I’ve always liked running water from the tap. A water dispenser is more of a compromise for me.

09.12.2023#

I always trust the guys, and this time I was not mistaken.

They quickly found a new place to live, though only for three months. But it wasn’t too expensive and had good renovations.

In general, just so you know, landlords usually don’t like tenants with pets very much.

To some extent, I always add difficulties in the search for housing. At such moments, I want to ask people: if you dislike pets so much, why do you breed us?

But the owner of this apartment was very kind and even met me once. We left her a deposit, which she returned to us later.

The area of the new apartment was small, but the bed there was huge, and it even had an orthopedic mattress. Bliss!

23.12.2023#

Life was going at a steady pace. In this area, the kids didn’t have many places to go for a walk, and they spent a lot of time with me again. I love spending time with them.

30.12.2023#

The New Year was approaching. The guys bought some branches and made a wreath out of them themselves. I heard that such a wreath costs thirty lari, but they made it for five. How resourceful they are!

They bought me a toy that I will love more than all the other toys.

17.01.2024#

We didn’t have time to look back, the weekend flew by.

Still, how much an Instagram-worthy interior boosts photos. However, that doesn’t change the fact that I am a very beautiful kitty.

25.01.2024#

We slowly started preparing for the trip to Armenia. Or maybe not just to Armenia. I was scheduled for another rabies vaccination.

Trips are, of course, stressful. But where would we be without them? Our rental was ending around March.

There was still time before departure, and I was catching and living every moment of the serene life in Didi Digomi.

Diagnosis – Lymphoma#

02.02.2024#

This is my diary, so I would like to be candid and share a sad story that happened to me.

One day I lost my appetite and completely stopped eating. This had happened before with cholecystitis, and we thought it was happening again.

However, when we arrived at the clinic, the doctors examined me for a long time. They did an ultrasound for a long time and even called in another doctor who was off duty. They carefully did another ultrasound and shrugged their shoulders.

Then Sasha insisted on an X-ray. I didn’t understand what was happening, but then I realized. They found a large tumor in my abdomen the size of a plum.

The doctors said: surgery is urgently needed, and the guys left me in the clinic for several hours. I was operated on and they removed this tumor from me.

Its size impressed us. You can see it in the photo, but I warn you: not for the faint-hearted!

At first, Sasha and Vita took the tumor and took it for examination. It was urgent to check whether it was malignant. Then they came back for me. I was just starting to come out of anesthesia. Anesthesia is a tough thing.

03.02.2024#

I needed to be monitored at first so that I wouldn’t accidentally fall out of bed, as I couldn’t manage myself properly.

I was very cold, and I was warmed up with homemade heating pads made from jars.

I was very weak and looked more like a rag than a kitty.

My caregivers were very worried about me, watching over me all night to ensure I wouldn’t hurt myself, for example, when I tried to go to the bathroom.

05.02.2024#

Sasha published some details about my surgery on his channel:

Sasha’s Publication:

Recently we vaccinated Myata, and then her condition worsened significantly.

At first, we thought her condition worsened due to the vaccinations, but when Myata had an ultrasound, they found a tumor the size of a plum. A follow-up X-ray confirmed it. In the first image, the huge tumor is clearly visible.

As a result, she underwent bowel resection, removing the affected area. I illustrated this on my crude diagram, you can take a look.

After the surgery, she underwent a course of IV drips. She was also given antibiotics, and today was the last of those procedures.

Before leaving Georgia, we will visit the clinic one more time. We will show ourselves.

Myata seems to be on the mend. However, she has lost weight, and for now, we are feeding her special wet food. But she is eating, and her strength is returning to her 🩷

We are lucky that the nearest clinic to us is “Didi Dogi.” They have very good reviews. However, we had to do the histology at another clinic, as it seems there is only one laboratory in Tbilisi that can determine the nature of tumors in animals.

In general, we are waiting for the histology results, which should take about 12 days.

Unfortunately, later our fears were confirmed, and the examination of the tumor showed that it was malignant. I was diagnosed with Large Cell Lymphoma (in short). I thought that, probably, with such a diagnosis, they would put me to sleep.

Chemotherapy#

08.02.2024#

Fortunately, I won’t be put to sleep. Meow.

Sasha was advised to see an oncologist. It seems that at that time, he was the only practicing veterinary oncologist — Grigory Skopintsev.

A decision was made to start treatment with chemotherapy. Expensive, unpleasant, and all for the chance to extend my life for a little while longer.

The oncologist’s forecasts were not encouraging. We were warned that even with chemotherapy, I might live only from six months to a year.

In an exceptional case — about two years. And without treatment — just two weeks.

11.02.2024#

After the surgery, I lost weight. I seem to have become slimmer. But in the future, we will still fight for this weight, because I will lose even more.

Sasha bought the necessary medications, and Grigory started therapy according to the “Madison Wisconsin Protocol.”

This protocol involves 19 weeks of therapy. Every three weeks, one had to take a blood test and administer a specific medication. At home, one had to take pills.

The medications are human. Not in the sense of humane, but in the sense that these same drugs are used by people to fight cancer.

25.02.2024#

Well, I recovered from the shock and got back to my old ways — started goofing around again.

And I didn’t care that there were no oncologists in Armenia, and we were going there soon. Sasha was dealing with those questions.

But Sasha still couldn’t find an oncologist after calling all the clinics he could find, so it was decided to look for a doctor on-site when we got there. Grigory agreed to help us with therapy remotely.

28.02.2024#

Meanwhile, the moment had come for us to head to Yerevan.

My “excitement” about the trip is very hard to hide. But we were traveling by private transfer, not by plane. I could walk a bit around the car’s cabin, look out the window, and meow loudly.

And I also described Vita.

Yerevan#

01.03.2024#

So. Now we live in Armenia. To be more precise, in Yerevan. I’ll let you in on a secret: at first, the guys wanted to live in Dilijan, but my everyday life had to be mixed with regular chemotherapy. So we needed the capital. The first thing the guys explored upon arriving in Yerevan was not tourist routes, but local veterinary clinics. It turned out to be not so simple.

Sasha called clinics, but everyone refused to perform an unfamiliar procedure for them, even under the guidance of a remote doctor.

In one of the clinics, we got the green light. First, Grigory consulted the veterinarian Ovanes, and he did everything necessary! A little later, we found a less busy department of the same clinic, and the procedures were done there.

03.03.2024#

I felt very unstable. Sometimes very good, sometimes very bad.

I became picky about food. I had no appetite, and at first, the guys didn’t know how to deal with it. So, hoping that I would like something, they bought a bunch of different food. And I even refused sour cream. Can you imagine a cat that refused fresh sour cream?

Take a look at the photo. This is us still in the hostel. The guys haven’t found housing yet, but they are already taking me to hospitals. Do you see how many food options there are?

And yes, my new water bowl is visible in the photo, which I haven’t told you about yet.

05.03.2024#

However, Sasha and Vita quickly found a new place to live (something between an apartment and a private house), and we settled in there for six months.

They made a little house for me in this house so that I could be in the little house when in the house.

Grigory (my remote doctor) taught Sasha how to administer treatment, and for some chemotherapy sessions, Sasha did it himself. It was a novelty for him, but I liked it more because I didn’t have to stress too much about trips to veterinary clinics.

The owners might have been scared to see a bunch of vials and syringes in our trash. But we warned them that a sick kitty lives in the house.

Overall, it was very nice that they let us stay without even asking for a separate deposit. The owners were named Ashkhen and Aik, and we liked them very much. One time, Sasha baked brownies for them, and they brought fresh mangoes for the kids from hot countries. In general, we lived there in harmony.

Here’s what Sasha wrote about it:

Sasha’s Publication

We conducted a chemotherapy session. And I did everything myself.

I bought gloves, a mask, the necessary medications, and saline solution. It turned out to be not easy to find the medications; they are not registered in Armenia.

I called the oncologist who consults us online, mixed everything needed with saline solution under his supervision. I did all the work under the hood, after which, of course, I washed everything. I administered it under the scruff.

In general, Myata even actively played with me today. Although just yesterday she almost ran away into the night Yerevan, darting out onto the street. I barely managed to catch her. And she meowed loudly all evening.

I won’t be able to administer the next medication myself. It is quite toxic and could harm Myata if, for example, I miss the vein.

A little later, I will try to show our treatment journal. I record everything in a Google spreadsheet.

08.03.2024#

If we set aside the treatment history, everything was very good! It seems this was the coziest place we lived in. The previous apartment was also very beautiful and somewhat similar to this one, but it was smaller and far from the center. And this one is not. I didn’t need the center, but the guys were happy.

17.03.2024#

The guys bought a camera again in Georgia. There was an unpleasant story in one of the apartments, and Sasha set up the camera to ensure safety.

Now everything was fine with the apartment, but not with me — not so much. It was very convenient to see how much I ate, how much I drank, and whether I had messed things up while they were away.

This is the video where I meet them.

26.03.2024#

My life has more or less stabilized. Only the mustache from chemotherapy has started to wobble. It seems like they are beginning to fall out.

10.04.2024#

In Yerevan, I spent almost the entire summer on the windowsill.

To prevent me from catching a cold from the stone windowsill, they laid a towel for me there. If only you knew how much I love lying on clean towels. I really enjoy it. I am a true neat freak.

And remember, I wrote that absolutely everyone notices my beautiful eyes when they see me? Well, it’s hard to convey their color in the photo, but it seems to have worked in this one. Look at my little eyes.

13.04.2024#

I started to get in shape and I have the strength and desire to play. I love playing with hair ties. Guests should not leave them unattended.

06.05.2024#

Chemotherapy has become a routine. It took longer to get to the department we chose, but there was no one there, and I was very calm, almost not stressed.

16.02.2026#

I have decided to bury Myata at the pet cemetery. Cremation turned out to be complicated, and the option offered through the government institution sounded quite dubious.

Thank you to everyone who supported me. In particular, to those who wrote to me personally and to those who reacted to my memorial reel with purring Myata https://www.instagram.com/p/DUz0inqjRwx/.

Alena, who met Myata even earlier than I did, upon learning of what happened, sent me a money transfer. I don’t need it, but I promised that I would arrange the grave and send her a scan of Myata’s paw prints, which were made at the clinic post-mortem. It hurts me so much to write about this.

How did this happen?

Myata was supposed to undergo a scheduled surgery to remove her gallbladder. She was feeling very well. But the removal was necessary.

I consulted several doctors before making the decision: there was a stone in the gallbladder measuring 1.2 centimeters.

I chose a clinic where the gallbladder removal could be performed using a minimally invasive method, i.e., without a large incision, using several punctures and a laparoscope. This is actually called laparoscopy.

Recovery after such an operation is significantly easier than after a classical one. We took tests, checked with a cardiologist, and no contraindications for the surgery were found.

There were a couple of problems, but I was told that these were not contraindications: Myata had elevated creatinine and slight, likely age-related, changes in her heart.

The surgery was planned for February 14, but due to electrical problems at the clinic, it was postponed to February 15.

They started operating on Myata after lunch. I don’t know exactly what time, but by 4:30 PM, I received a call saying that she had died.

During the surgery, or rather almost at the very beginning, Myata experienced severe arrhythmia that could not be stopped, so her heart could not withstand it.

That is, she died from the anesthesia.

I can only speculate how this happened. Perhaps her kidneys were working under strain, and therefore there was an electrolyte imbalance in her body. And maybe potassium should have been checked before the surgery.

Maybe the anesthesia itself or its dosage was fatal: as I understand, ketamine, diazepam, or both were used.

I don’t know.

Recently, especially after her dental treatment, Myata looked happy. She purred in my arms.

After the burial, I marked the place with stones a bit. I plan to beautify this place better.

Goodbye, Myata.
