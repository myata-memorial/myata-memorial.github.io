## Diagnosis – Lymphoma

### 02.02.2024

This is my diary, so I would like to be candid and share a sad story that happened to me.

One day I lost my appetite and completely stopped eating. This had happened before with cholecystitis, and we thought it was happening again.

However, when we arrived at the clinic, the doctors examined me for a long time. They did an ultrasound for a long time and even called in another doctor who was off duty. They carefully did another ultrasound and shrugged their shoulders.

Then Sasha insisted on an X-ray. I didn't understand what was happening, but then I realized. They found a large tumor in my abdomen the size of a plum.

The doctors said: surgery is urgently needed, and the guys left me in the clinic for several hours. I was operated on and they removed this tumor from me.

Its size impressed us. You can see it in the photo, but I warn you: not for the faint-hearted!

At first, Sasha and Vita took the tumor and took it for examination. It was urgent to check whether it was malignant. Then they came back for me. I was just starting to come out of anesthesia. Anesthesia is a tough thing.

## Death

### 15.02.2026

Hello, this is Sasha.

I have a burning sensation under my ribs and tears are flowing. Today, on Sunday, February 15, 2026, at around 16:30, Myata passed away.

I loved her very much and will continue to love her. She will remain in my heart forever.

The doctor called me when I was at the café near Tsminda Sameba, waiting for a call with news that the surgery had gone well, but he called with different news. I arrived at the clinic, and they told me what had happened.

I was allowed to see Myata; she was lying on the operating table, wrapped in a white blanket, and seemed to be peacefully sleeping, gently closing her eyes. She looked relaxed and not at all like she was dead. I kissed her one last time.

### 16.02.2026

I have decided to bury Myata at the pet cemetery. Cremation turned out to be complicated, and the option offered through the government institution sounded quite dubious.

Thank you to everyone who supported me. In particular, to those who wrote to me personally and to those who reacted to my memorial reel with purring Myata [https://www.instagram.com/p/DUz0inqjRwx/](https://www.instagram.com/p/DUz0inqjRwx/).

Alena, who met Myata even earlier than I did, upon learning of what happened, sent me a money transfer. I don't need it, but I promised that I would arrange the grave and send her a scan of Myata's paw prints, which were made at the clinic post-mortem. It hurts me so much to write about this.

How did this happen?

Myata was supposed to undergo a scheduled surgery to remove her gallbladder. She was feeling very well. But the removal was necessary.

I consulted several doctors before making the decision: there was a stone in the gallbladder measuring 1.2 centimeters.

I chose a clinic where the gallbladder removal could be performed using a minimally invasive method, i.e., without a large incision, using several punctures and a laparoscope. This is actually called laparoscopy.

Recovery after such an operation is significantly easier than after a classical one. We took tests, checked with a cardiologist, and no contraindications for the surgery were found.

There were a couple of problems, but I was told that these were not contraindications: Myata had elevated creatinine and slight, likely age-related, changes in her heart.

The surgery was planned for February 14, but due to electrical problems at the clinic, it was postponed to February 15.

They started operating on Myata after lunch. I don't know exactly what time, but by 4:30 PM, I received a call saying that she had died.

During the surgery, or rather almost at the very beginning, Myata experienced severe arrhythmia that could not be stopped, so her heart could not withstand it.

That is, she died from the anesthesia.

I can only speculate how this happened. Perhaps her kidneys were working under strain, and therefore there was an electrolyte imbalance in her body. And maybe potassium should have been checked before the surgery.

Maybe the anesthesia itself or its dosage was fatal: as I understand, ketamine, diazepam, or both were used.

I don't know.

Recently, especially after her dental treatment, Myata looked happy. She purred in my arms.

After the burial, I marked the place with stones a bit. I plan to beautify this place better.

Goodbye, Myata.
